Graton Gathright

Product Vision & Backend Engineering

Graton is an economist and applied scientist who spent seven years measuring the causal impact of Amazon's marketing, then helped build the science team at Haus. He holds a PhD in Economics from UC San Diego. On the water, he's an ACA Level 3 Coastal Kayaking Instructor and Body Boat Blade coach who thrives in tide races, surf zones, and rock gardens along Washington's coast and Vancouver Island. He also loves "noodling" — finessing his kayak through tight spaces with precision and control. Graton leads product design and system architecture for Current Events, owns the backend engineering, and manages 3 Raccoons' business operations. The product grew out of a vision he's had for years — a single tool built around how sea kayakers actually plan trips.

Erika O'Leary

Business Strategy & UX

Erika is a chiropractor and sea kayaking instructor based in Bremerton, Washington. She grew up paddling the Niangua River and boating in the Ozarks before moving to the Pacific Northwest, where Audrey Sutherland's Paddling North sparked a deep commitment to sea kayaking. She has explored much of Puget Sound and developed a particular love for the dynamic waters of the Strait of Juan de Fuca. An ACA Level 3 Coastal Kayaking Instructor and Body Boat Blade coach, Erika has taught for the Mountaineers and North Sound Sea Kayaking Association and leads the weekly Tuesday Skills Practice series in Kitsap. At 3 Raccoons, she consults on product vision, user experience, and business operations.

Asger Pjetursson

Product Design & Frontend Engineering

Asger is a software developer and lifelong paddler from Denmark. He holds a BA in Philosophy from Aarhus University and is completing an MS in Software Design at the IT University of Copenhagen. Before returning to school, he managed BestCoast Outfitters — a kayak equipment shop in Victoria, BC — where his work earned the store a finalist spot for Business of the Year from the Victoria Chamber of Commerce. He previously sold kayaking gear across Scandinavia for TeamKayak and is a Paddle Canada Level 2 Instructor who has guided sea kayak expeditions in Antarctica. Asger is a full partner on product design and leads the front-end engineering for Current Events, shaping the UI and user experience.
